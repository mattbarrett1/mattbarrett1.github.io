Resonate

Written and created by "justquant", Resonate is a seemingly harmless virus created as more of a joke rather than
a real threat. While the original source code was taken down by the author, a user by the name of "RecreatingResonate"
reuploaded it to Github, or at least what was left of it.

As mentioned previously, Resonate wasn't created to cause real harm. I included this to show that there exists a small
group of people who create viruses simply out of fun, and to flex their power to other hackers. Most early malware was
built this way. For example, Creeper, which was believed to be the first virus, simply displayed the phrase
I AM THE CREEPER CATCH ME IF YOU CAN
without causing any real damage.

Resonate will infect files upon being run, like most viruses. However, the interesting aspects of Resonate activate
only on certian date. These are known as "payloads", which are used to describe sets of instructions carried out by viruses.
Notable resonate payloads include causing icons to explode when clicked, playing an endless loop of the Super Mario Brothers 3 Theme,
or covering the entire screen with a pixelated image of Fred Durst (See image below)

Source

As mentioned previously, Resonate may cause some damage to systems by overwritting files, but it wasn't created maliciously
and the only way a system can be infected is if the user voluntarily downloads the virus itself.
