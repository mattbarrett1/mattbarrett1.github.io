WannaCry, or WannaCrypt, was a ransomware attack that started in 2017. While the author is assumed to be a Korean
hacker group known as the Lazarus Group, it has yet to be officially confirmed. While the initial attack only lasted
around one day, WannaCry was estimated to infect 300,000 computers.

WannaCry is a ransomeware, which acts differently to other malware. Instead of overwritting files, ransomeware will encrypt
files or sensitive information and demand payment in order for the files to be decryped. In most cases, including WannaCry,
the payment is through cryptocurrency.

When WannaCry infects a computer, it quickly encrypts all files it can access. A pop up will then be displayed, explaining
the situation and providing a bitcoin address.

Source

WannaCry abused a vulnerability in the SMB protocol of Windows. While there was a patch provided by Microsoft prior to
the attack, many computers weren't up to date.

The attack only lasted a few hours, as a man named Marcus Hutchins discovered that purhcasing the domain that the malware
was tied to acted as a killswitch, effectively stopping the virus from spreading to other computers in the area. While he was
initally praised for his efforts, the large about of attention led the police to eventually discovering his involvement with
MalwareTech, leaning to his arrest.
